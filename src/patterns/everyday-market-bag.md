# The Everyday Market Bag

**A free pattern from Purl of Wisdom**

A sturdy, stretchy market bag that's as satisfying to knit as it is to use. This is the bag you'll reach for every time you head to the farmers' market, the yarn shop, or the library. Knit in worsted-weight cotton, it stretches to hold a surprising amount and folds down to practically nothing in your purse.

**Designed by Inna for the Purl of Wisdom community.**

## Pattern Details

| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| **Skill level** | Confident beginner to intermediate |
| **Finished size** | Approx. 14" wide × 15" tall (before stretching), excluding handles |
| **Yarn** | Worsted-weight cotton or cotton blend — approx. 250 yards / 230 metres (1–2 skeins depending on put-up) |
| **Suggested yarns** | Drops Paris, Paintbox Yarns Cotton Aran, Lion Brand 24/7 Cotton |
| **Needles** | US 7 (4.5 mm) circular, 16" and 24" lengths; US 7 DPNs or preferred method for small-circumference knitting |
| **Notions** | Stitch marker, tapestry needle, scrap yarn or stitch holder |
| **Gauge** | 18 stitches × 24 rows = 4" (10 cm) in stockinette stitch, after blocking |

> **Gauge matters here!** A looser gauge gives a stretchier bag but larger holes in the mesh section. A tighter gauge makes a denser fabric. Swatch in the round if possible, since your circular gauge may differ from flat.

## Pattern Notes

- This bag is knit **bottom-up in the round** — no seaming required.
- The base is worked flat and then joined for knitting in the round.
- The mesh section creates the stretch. If you prefer a solid bag, simply work stockinette instead of the mesh pattern.
- **Blocking is essential.** Wet-block the finished bag aggressively, stretching it over a box or bag form to open up the mesh and set the shape.

## Instructions

### Base

Using the 24" circular needle (or DPNs), CO 56 sts.

Work back and forth in garter stitch (knit every row) for 20 rows (10 garter ridges).

**Pick up for working in the round:**

1. Knit across the 56 live stitches.
2. Pick up and knit 10 sts along the short side edge (1 st per garter ridge).
3. Pick up and knit 56 sts along the cast-on edge.
4. Pick up and knit 10 sts along the remaining short side edge.
5. PM and join for working in the round. — **132 sts total.**

### Transition Rounds

- **Rnds 1–6:** Knit all stitches (stockinette in the round).

### Mesh Body

The mesh section creates the stretchy, open fabric that makes this bag so practical.

**Mesh Pattern (4-round repeat):**

- **Rnd 1:** \*K2tog, YO\* — repeat to end of round.
- **Rnd 2:** Knit all stitches.
- **Rnd 3:** \*YO, SSK\* — repeat to end of round.
- **Rnd 4:** Knit all stitches.

Repeat these 4 rounds **15 times** (60 rounds total), or until the bag body measures approximately 13" from the base.

### Top Border

- **Rnds 1–8:** Knit all stitches (stockinette).
- **Rnd 9:** Purl all stitches (creates a turning ridge).
- **Rnds 10–16:** Knit all stitches.

**Do not bind off.** Fold the top border to the inside at the purl ridge and use a tapestry needle to whip-stitch the live stitches to the inside of the bag, creating a clean, reinforced top edge.

### Handles (Make 2)

**First handle:**
Starting 14 sts from the right side of the front of the bag, pick up and knit 28 sts along the top edge (through both layers of the folded border).

- **Row 1 (WS):** Purl all stitches.
- **Row 2 (RS):** K1, SSK, knit to last 3 sts, K2tog, K1. — (26 sts)
- **Row 3:** Purl.
- **Row 4:** K1, SSK, knit to last 3 sts, K2tog, K1. — (24 sts)
- **Row 5:** Purl.
- **Row 6:** Knit.

Continue in stockinette (knit RS, purl WS) until the handle measures **10 inches** from the pick-up row, or desired length.

**BO all stitches.**

**Second handle:**
Repeat on the back of the bag, mirroring the position of the first handle.

### Finishing

1. Fold each handle in half lengthwise, wrong sides together.
2. Use mattress stitch or whip stitch to seam the long edges of each handle, creating a sturdy tube.
3. Attach the bound-off end of each handle to the opposite side of the bag top, matching the pick-up position. Sew securely with a tapestry needle — these handles will bear weight!
4. Weave in all ends.

## Blocking

Soak the bag in lukewarm water with a splash of wool wash (even for cotton — it softens the fabric) for 20 minutes. Squeeze gently — do not wring. Stretch the bag over a rectangular form (a shoebox works perfectly) and allow to dry completely. This opens up the mesh and sets the shape.

## Tips and Variations

- **Solid bag:** Skip the mesh section entirely and work stockinette throughout for a denser, non-stretch bag. You may want to add 2–3 inches to the body length.
- **Colourwork:** The stockinette transition and top border are great places to add a stripe or two of a contrast colour.
- **Longer handles:** For over-the-shoulder carry, work the handles to 16–18 inches before binding off.
- **Reinforcement:** For extra-heavy loads, hold a strand of linen yarn alongside your cotton when working the handles.
