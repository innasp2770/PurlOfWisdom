# Cross-Promotion Strategy — Purl of Wisdom

**Goal:** Partner with 5 complementary knitting/craft newsletters for mutual cross-promotion swaps
**Timeline:** Phase 2–3 of growth plan (Weeks 5–12)
**Expected impact:** 20–50 new subscribers per successful swap

---

## Target Newsletters

### 1. The Fiber Fold
- **Niche:** Crochet patterns and tutorials
- **Platform:** Beehiiv
- **Estimated audience:** 1,000–3,000 subscribers
- **Why they're a fit:** Adjacent craft with significant audience overlap — many subscribers knit *and* crochet. Non-competitive since we focus on knitting.
- **Swap proposal:** We feature their best beginner crochet pattern; they mention Purl of Wisdom as a sister newsletter for knitters.
- **Status:** To contact

### 2. Slow Stitch Collective
- **Niche:** Slow fashion, mending, and handmade garments
- **Platform:** Substack / Beehiiv
- **Estimated audience:** 2,000–5,000 subscribers
- **Why they're a fit:** Shared values around intentional making and quality over fast fashion. Their readers are likely interested in knitting as a craft practice.
- **Swap proposal:** We write a short piece on "knitting your own wardrobe basics"; they feature us as a resource for readers wanting to learn knitting.
- **Status:** To contact

### 3. Dye Day Weekly
- **Niche:** Indie-dyed yarn reviews, colorways, and fiber arts
- **Platform:** Beehiiv
- **Estimated audience:** 500–2,000 subscribers
- **Why they're a fit:** Their audience already buys yarn and is deeply engaged in the fiber arts world. Perfect overlap with our intermediate knitter audience.
- **Swap proposal:** We spotlight their favourite spring colorways; they recommend Purl of Wisdom for technique and pattern content.
- **Status:** To contact

### 4. The Pattern Pages
- **Niche:** Weekly roundup of free and affordable knitting/crochet patterns
- **Platform:** Beehiiv / Mailchimp
- **Estimated audience:** 1,500–4,000 subscribers
- **Why they're a fit:** Direct audience match — their readers want patterns and would love our technique tips to execute them better.
- **Swap proposal:** We recommend their weekly roundup as a companion resource; they feature our "Stitch of the Week" as a skill-building complement.
- **Status:** To contact

### 5. Make & Mend Monthly
- **Niche:** Sustainable crafting, upcycling, textile arts
- **Platform:** Substack
- **Estimated audience:** 1,000–3,000 subscribers
- **Why they're a fit:** Eco-conscious makers who value handcraft. Knitting fits naturally into their sustainability ethos.
- **Swap proposal:** We write about sustainable yarn choices; they introduce Purl of Wisdom as the go-to knitting newsletter for mindful makers.
- **Status:** To contact

## Swap Execution Checklist

For each confirmed swap:

- [ ] Agree on timing (same week ideally, or within 2 weeks)
- [ ] Draft our feature blurb (2–3 sentences, genuine recommendation)
- [ ] Review their draft about us (ensure accurate description)
- [ ] Publish and notify partner when live
- [ ] Track signups from the swap (use UTM link: `?utm_source=crosspromo&utm_medium=newsletter&utm_campaign=[partner-name]`)
- [ ] Send a thank-you note after and share results

## Finding More Partners

**Where to look:**
- Beehiiv Discover page → search "knitting", "crochet", "fiber arts", "crafts"
- Substack → browse "Crafts" and "Hobbies" categories
- Newsletter Stack → search craft-related keywords
- Ask existing swap partners for introductions

**Criteria for good partners:**
- 500–5,000 subscribers (similar scale to us)
- Active publication schedule (at least bi-weekly)
- Engaged audience (they reply to their readers, have comments)
- Non-competitive but adjacent content
- Genuine quality — we only recommend newsletters we'd actually read
