# Beehiiv Boost — Application & Strategy

## What is Beehiiv Boost?

Beehiiv Boost is a paid recommendation network where newsletters recommend each other to their subscribers. When a reader subscribes to another newsletter in the Boost network, the recommending newsletter earns a payout per subscriber. Conversely, you can pay to have other newsletters recommend *yours*.

**For Purl of Wisdom, Boost is an acquisition channel** — we pay a small amount per new subscriber gained through recommendations from other Beehiiv newsletters.

---

## Application Requirements

To qualify for Beehiiv Boost, newsletters typically need:

1. **Active Beehiiv publication** — must be publishing on Beehiiv (we are)
2. **Minimum subscriber count** — Beehiiv generally requires 1,000+ subscribers, though smaller newsletters in underserved niches may be accepted
3. **Consistent publishing schedule** — at least weekly (we publish weekly)
4. **Healthy engagement metrics** — open rates above 30% (our target: >45%)
5. **Beehiiv Scale or Max plan** — Boost is available on paid plans

## Application Steps

### Step 1: Verify Plan Eligibility

1. Log into Beehiiv Dashboard
2. Navigate to Settings → Billing
3. Confirm we are on the **Scale** or **Max** plan (Boost requires paid plan)
4. If on a lower tier, evaluate upgrade cost vs. expected subscriber acquisition ROI

### Step 2: Apply to the Boost Network

1. Navigate to: **Beehiiv Dashboard → Monetize → Boosts**
2. Click **"Apply to Boost Network"** (or "Get Started" if first time)
3. Fill in the application:
   - **Newsletter description:** "Purl of Wisdom is a weekly knitting newsletter for intermediate knitters who want to level up their skills. Each issue features technique deep-dives, yarn reviews, pattern tips, and the knitting stories behind the stitches."
   - **Target audience:** Knitters, crafters, makers, fiber arts enthusiasts
   - **Publishing frequency:** Weekly
   - **Current metrics:** Share open rate, subscriber count, and click rate
4. Submit and wait for approval (typically 1–5 business days)

### Step 3: Configure Boost Settings (After Approval)

1. **Set your cost-per-acquisition (CPA):**
   - Recommended starting CPA: **$1.00–$2.00 per subscriber**
   - This is competitive for a niche newsletter — craft/maker audiences are engaged and retain well
2. **Set a daily or monthly budget cap:**
   - **Recommended starting budget:** $50–$100/month
   - Start conservative; scale up based on subscriber quality and retention
3. **Set targeting preferences:**
   - Target newsletters in: crafts, DIY, lifestyle, creativity, maker culture
   - Exclude: finance, crypto, tech (low audience overlap)
4. **Write your recommendation card copy:**
   - **Headline:** "Purl of Wisdom"
   - **Description:** "Weekly knitting tips, technique deep-dives, and yarn wisdom for makers who want to knit smarter. Join the community."
   - **Logo:** Upload Purl of Wisdom logo/wordmark

### Step 4: Monitor and Optimize

| Metric | Target | Action if off-target |
|---|---|---|
| Cost per subscriber | < $2.00 | Lower CPA bid or tighten targeting |
| 30-day retention | > 80% | Review welcome sequence; are Boost subscribers getting value? |
| Open rate (Boost cohort) | > 35% | Segment and compare to organic subscribers |
| Monthly spend | $50–$100 | Pause if retention drops below 70% |

**Check weekly:** Beehiiv Dashboard → Monetize → Boosts → Analytics

## Budget Recommendation

| Phase | Monthly Budget | Expected Subscribers | CPA Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Test (Month 1)** | $50 | 25–50 | $1.00–$2.00 |
| **Scale (Month 2–3)** | $100 | 50–100 | $1.00–$2.00 |
| **Optimize (Month 4+)** | Adjust based on ROI | — | Maintain <$2.00 |

**Total Phase 3 budget:** $50–$100 (conservative, within growth plan parameters)

**ROI consideration:** If our newsletter generates revenue through sponsorships at $50+ CPM, each subscriber is worth ~$0.60/year in sponsorship revenue alone at current rates. At $2 CPA, payback period is ~3 years on sponsorship alone — but subscriber lifetime value increases significantly with paid tier and digital products. Start small, prove retention, then scale.
