# Influencer Outreach Strategy — Purl of Wisdom

**Goal:** Partner with 3–5 knitting influencers on Instagram (5k–50k followers) to drive newsletter signups
**Timeline:** Phase 2–3 of growth plan (Weeks 5–12)
**Expected impact:** 30–100 new subscribers per successful collaboration

---

## Target Influencers

### 1. @woolandwonder
- **Followers:** ~12k
- **Content style:** Colourful sweater knitting, WIP updates, yarn hauls
- **Why they're a fit:** Engaged community of intermediate knitters who love learning new techniques. Posts get strong comments and saves.
- **Collaboration idea:** Feature them in a "Maker Spotlight" newsletter issue; they share the issue in Stories with a swipe-up to subscribe.
- **Status:** To contact

### 2. @stitchandtell
- **Followers:** ~25k
- **Content style:** Knitting tutorials (Reels), pattern reviews, beginner-friendly content
- **Why they're a fit:** Tutorial-focused content aligns perfectly with our educational angle. Their audience wants to learn — exactly our reader profile.
- **Collaboration idea:** Co-create a "5 Stitches Every Knitter Should Know" Reel; we provide the written deep-dive in the newsletter, they post the video.
- **Status:** To contact

### 3. @slowknitsclub
- **Followers:** ~8k
- **Content style:** Mindful knitting, slow fashion, earth-toned aesthetic
- **Why they're a fit:** Values-aligned audience that appreciates depth and quality over speed. Our long-form newsletter content complements their visual feed.
- **Collaboration idea:** We write a piece on "The case for slow knitting"; they share it as a Story series with their personal take.
- **Status:** To contact

### 4. @yarnovereverything
- **Followers:** ~35k
- **Content style:** Pattern roundups, yarn reviews, knitting humour
- **Why they're a fit:** High engagement, active community, and a fun tone that matches our approachable voice. Their audience regularly asks for recommendations.
- **Collaboration idea:** Provide an exclusive "Tip of the Week" for their Stories; they tag us and link to the newsletter for the full version.
- **Status:** To contact

### 5. @knitpurlrepeat_
- **Followers:** ~6k
- **Content style:** Project diaries, technique close-ups, yarn dyeing
- **Why they're a fit:** Smaller but highly engaged niche audience. Great for authentic partnership since they're still growing too.
- **Collaboration idea:** Newsletter feature on their dyeing process + their favourite techniques; they share with their community.
- **Status:** To contact

## Collaboration Formats (Menu of Options)

Offer these as options — let the influencer pick what feels most natural:

| Format | Our Effort | Their Effort | Expected Impact |
|--------|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Maker Spotlight** | Write a feature about them in our newsletter | Share the issue in Stories + link to subscribe | High — personal endorsement |
| **Co-created content** | Write the long-form version of a technique | Post the visual version (Reel/carousel) and tag us | High — content lives in both places |
| **Exclusive tip share** | Provide weekly "Tip of the Week" text + graphic | Post in Stories with newsletter link | Medium — ongoing relationship |
| **Pattern collaboration** | Feature their pattern in our newsletter with review | Mention the review to their audience | Medium — mutual value |
| **Giveaway** | Provide newsletter subscription as part of prize package | Host the giveaway on their feed | Medium-High — drives signups |

## Outreach Best Practices

- **Do your homework.** Reference a specific project or post they shared. Generic messages get ignored.
- **Lead with value.** What do *they* get out of this? Feature in our newsletter, content for their feed, access to our audience.
- **Keep it short.** DMs should be 3–4 sentences max. Save details for the follow-up.
- **Be patient.** Influencers are busy. Follow up once after 5–7 days, then let it go.
- **Never be transactional.** Engage with their content genuinely before reaching out. Like and comment for 1–2 weeks first.
- **Respect "no."** A decline is not a door closing — it's "not right now." Stay friendly.

## Pre-Outreach Checklist

Before contacting any influencer:

- [ ] Follow their account and engage genuinely for 1–2 weeks
- [ ] Read their recent content to identify a specific compliment
- [ ] Confirm they haven't recently done a similar collab with a competitor
- [ ] Prepare our "media kit" — 2–3 recent newsletter issues + subscriber count + open rate
- [ ] Have the specific collaboration idea ready (not vague "let's collab")
