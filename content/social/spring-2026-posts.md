## Post 3: Knitting Tip — Myth-Busting

**Platform:** Instagram (single image or Reel still)

**Visual concept:** Bold text-based graphic on a warm cream background. Main text: "MYTH: You have to knit tight to get even stitches." Below it, in a contrasting color: "TRUTH: Consistent tension matters more than tight tension." A small illustration of hands holding needles with relaxed grip in the corner. Branded with Purl of Wisdom at the bottom.

**Caption:**
Let's bust a knitting myth that trips up so many beginners (and even some experienced knitters!).

Tight stitches ≠ even stitches.

When you grip the yarn too hard, you end up with sore hands, slow progress, and fabric that feels stiff instead of drapey. The real secret? **Consistency.** Keep your tension steady — whether it's loose, medium, or snug — and your stitches will even out beautifully over rows.

A few things that help:
→ Relax your grip (seriously, unclench those hands)
→ Let the needles do the work
→ Practice with a smooth, light-colored yarn so you can see your stitches clearly

Save this for the next time your inner critic says your stitches aren't "tight enough."
